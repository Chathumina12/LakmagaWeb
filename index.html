02 — The Problem

Why This Matters

Students in Sri Lanka face difficulties in understanding abstract STEM concepts due to limited access to practical learning environments and an over-reliance on traditional teaching methods.

Rote-Based Learning

Over-reliance on memorization leaves students unable to apply scientific concepts practically.

No Local Context

Existing VR tools are built on Western curricula with zero cultural relevance to Sri Lankan students.

Access Barriers

Rural schools lack lab equipment and internet access needed for modern interactive STEM education.

Declining Interest

Student engagement with STEM drops by 35% in middle school years due to abstract, disconnected curricula.

03 — The Solution

How Lakmaga Works

A seamless educational pipeline from headset to classroom analytics.

1Enter VR
2Interact
3Feedback
4Analytics
STEP 01

Enter the VR Environment

Students equip the Meta Quest 3 headset and are instantly transported into a historically reconstructed ancient Sigiriya village. The environment is designed with archaeological accuracy and serves as the contextual backdrop for all STEM learning modules.

STEP 02

Interact with STEM Tasks

Engage physically with 3D objects to solve curriculum-aligned puzzles. From calculating irrigation flow rates at the sluice gates to balancing merchant scales using algebraic equations, every task maps directly to national syllabus objectives.

STEP 03

Receive Real-Time Feedback

The gamification engine provides immediate in-world responses. Correct answers trigger visual rewards and progression. Mistakes generate guided hints, not punishments, fostering a growth mindset and reducing math anxiety.

STEP 04

Performance Telemetry

A passive telemetry system tracks every interaction without disrupting the learning flow. Educators receive detailed dashboards showing task completion rates, time-on-task, error patterns, and individual student struggle points.

06 — Research & Validation

Academic Foundation

Literature Survey

Research Background

Recent advancements in immersive technologies have demonstrated significant improvements in student engagement and knowledge retention. Virtual reality enables learners to interact with abstract concepts in a spatial environment, reducing cognitive load. However, existing solutions are largely based on Western curricula and lack contextual relevance for Sri Lankan students.

Methodology

Design-Based Research

The project follows an iterative Design-Based Research approach. The system was developed using Unity 3D and deployed on Meta Quest 3 hardware. Interactive modules were designed based on curriculum mapping and integrated with gamification elements including tasks, rewards, and feedback mechanisms.

Objectives

Research Goals

1. Design a gamified VR platform aligned with national curriculum.
2. Improve student engagement through immersive experiences.
3. Enhance conceptual understanding and retention.
4. Provide a culturally relevant educational environment.

07 — Commercialization & Future

Looking Forward

Beyond the academic prototype — pathways to real-world deployment and sustainable educational impact.

Target Institutions

Government and private middle schools across Sri Lanka, with initial pilot deployments targeting schools in Western Province and Central Province.

Market Relevance

The EdTech VR market in South Asia is projected to grow 32% annually. Lakmaga positions itself as the first curriculum-aligned VR platform for Sri Lankan STEM education.

Deployment Pathway

Phase 1: Pilot testing in 5 partner schools. Phase 2: Ministry of Education integration proposal. Phase 3: Commercial licensing to international schools.

Long-Term Sustainability

Modular content architecture enables continuous curriculum updates. Subscription-based licensing ensures ongoing development funding and teacher training programs.
