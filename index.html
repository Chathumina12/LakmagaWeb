02 — The Problem

Why This Matters

Students in Sri Lanka face difficulties in understanding abstract mathematical concepts like fractions, simultaneous equations, and volume calculations due to a lack of visual-spatial tools and cultural relevance.

Abstract Mathematics

Traditional classrooms fail to build visual-spatial intuition for fractions, equations, and spatial volumes.

No Cultural Context

Existing VR tools are localized for Western contexts, making them culturally foreign and less engaging for Sri Lankan students.

Mathematics Anxiety

Middle schoolers experience high mathematics anxiety, leading to a 35% decline in interest and performance.

Assessment Disruption

Traditional testing disrupts the learning flow and induces stress; existing tools lack seamless, stealthy behavioral telemetry.

03 — The Solution

How the Mathematics Works

A seamless educational pipeline from headset interaction to classroom performance telemetry.

STEP 01

Enter the VR Environment

Students equip the Meta Quest 3 headset and enter an archaeologically reconstructed ancient Sri Lankan village (Sigiriya era) serving as the contextual backdrop.

STEP 02

Interact with Math Tasks

Engage physically with 3D models to solve curriculum-aligned math tasks: Sluice Gates (fractions), Balance Scales (equations), and Excavations (volume).

STEP 03

Receive Real-Time Scaffolding

A three-outcome feedback system (visual, audio, and avatar-guided prompts) scaffolds learning and reduces math anxiety without penalties.

STEP 04

Stealth Telemetry Tracking

Passively logs student interactions, calculations, and errors into a Teacher Dashboard via CSV telemetry for real-time progress assessment.

04 — Core Mathematics Modules

Interactive VR Math Tasks

Curriculum-aligned mathematical operations embedded within historic Sri Lankan settings.

Grade 7 • Fractions & Decimals

Sluice Gate Fractions

Bisokotuwa Irrigation

Students regulate water levels in a reservoir by adjusting the sluice gate opening to match target fractions. This task transforms abstract fraction values into physical, spatial height proportions.

Pedagogical Insight: Visual-spatial scaling helps students understand fraction equivalence and magnitude.
Grade 8 • Simultaneous Equations

Merchant Balance Scale

Ancient Spice Trade

Set in a bustling heritage marketplace, students balance double-pan scales by solving for unknown weights of spice bags. Solve systems of equations physically by adding or removing tactile weights.

Pedagogical Insight: Models algebraic equality through the physical concept of static balance.
Grade 6-7 • Volume of Solids

Pit Excavation Volume

Granary Construction

Students measure excavations using traditional measuring rods, calculating volumes (L × W × H) to determine the exact soil displacement and how many baskets are needed to clear the site.

Pedagogical Insight: Reinforces 3D volume visualization and division using discrete physical units.

07 — Research & Validation

Academic Foundation

Literature Survey

Research Background

Virtual reality enables middle school learners to interact with abstract mathematical concepts in a spatial environment, reducing cognitive load and math anxiety. However, existing commercial platforms lack contextual relevance to local South Asian school curricula, and fail to provide non-disruptive, stealthy behavioral telemetry to teachers.

Methodology

Design Science Research

Grounding design in Situated Learning Theory and Stealth Assessment framework, the platform was developed using Unity 2022 LTS, C#, and Meta Quest 3 hardware. User performance data (calculations, errors, task timings) is logged passively to CSV logs via a custom achievement and ProgressTracker system.

Objectives

Research Goals

1. Design a gamified VR environment showcasing Sri Lankan heritage.
2. Implement curriculum-aligned tasks (fractions, equations, volume).
3. Establish a non-disruptive stealth assessment system.
4. Evaluate usability, engagement, and effectiveness in a pilot school.
